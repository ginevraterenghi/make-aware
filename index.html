What is antimicrobial resistance?

Definition

Antimicrobial resistance is the ability of bacteria to resist antibiotics. Bacteria develop the ability to survive the exposure to antibiotics designed to kill them, making the treatment last longer or even impossible, in some cases.

Definition

Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) occurs when bacteria, viruses, fungi and parasites change over time and no longer respond to medicines making infections harder to treat and increasing the risk of disease spread, severe illness and death.
